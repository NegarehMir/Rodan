Rodan is a web-based document recognition system; A workflow engine, developed by the Distributed Digital Music Archives Libraries (DDMAL) lab in McGill university. DDMAL is a part of the Single Interface for Music Score Searching and Analysis (SIMSSA) team which is divided into two axes: Content and Analysis. Rodanâ€™s job is to help bring these axes together.

Rodan manages workflows and monitors their status. Each workflow consists of one or more pluggable jobs and a status (scheduled, processing, finished, failed, canceled, waiting for input, etc). Each job can have one or more inputs/outputs.

Feedback

If you find Rodan useful and are using it in any deployments (large or small), please let us know. This work is part of our research, and keeping in touch about pilot projects or full-scale deployments helps us figure out what kinds of tools are valuable in digital library communities.

Sponsors

Diva.js didn't spring up fully formed overnight. It's been an ongoing project at the Distributed Digital Music Archives and Libraries Lab (DDMAL), at the Schulich School of Music of McGill University. It began as a collaboration with the Swiss RISM as part of a project funded by the Swiss National Science Foundation. Currently it is being developed as part of a three-year grant from the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada (SSHRC). We're also grateful for support from the Canadian Foundation for Innovation and the Centre for Interdisciplinary Research in Music Media and Technology (CIRMMT).
